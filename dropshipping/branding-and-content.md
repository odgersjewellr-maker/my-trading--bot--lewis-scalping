# Store Branding & Content Angles

*Phase 2 prep. Last updated: 16 July 2026.*

## 1. Store naming

Requirements for a branded general store:
- Broad enough to sell travel gear, fitness tools, and pet products under one roof
- Short, easy to spell after hearing it once, no hyphens or numbers
- Sounds like a brand, not a marketplace ("Shoply Deals Warehouse" = instant distrust)
- `.com` or `.com.au` available (note: .com.au requires an ABN — get that first)

### How to check a name (do these in order, ~15 minutes, all free)

1. **Domain**: search the name on a registrar (Namecheap for .com, VentraIP for
   .com.au). If the exact .com.au is free, that's the green light — .com.au
   registration needs your ABN, which you already have.
2. **Trademark**: search.ipaustralia.gov.au → search the name under classes 35
   (retail) and your product classes. An identical/near-identical live mark in
   those classes = pick another name.
3. **ASIC business name**: connectonline.asic.gov.au → "Check name availability".
   Registering costs ~AU$45/yr and is required if you trade under the name.
4. **Social handles**: search the exact name on TikTok and Instagram. You want
   the clean handle (@sunnyroo, not @sunnyroo_au_official).

If all four are clear: buy the domain and register the handles the same day —
names get taken, and the domain is the cheapest insurance (~AU$25).

### Brand basics (keep it simple)

- One accent colour + black/white. Clean sans-serif logo (Canva free tier is fine).
- Tagline pattern that fits a discovery store: "Finds that make life easier."
- Store pages needed at launch: Home, 3 product pages, About, Contact,
  Shipping, Refunds, Privacy, Terms (Shopify generates the last four).

## 2. TikTok content angles (organic, £0)

Rules that apply to all three products:
- Post 1–3 videos/day per product account. Expect most to flop — that's normal;
  you're buying lottery tickets until one hits the algorithm.
- First 1.5 seconds decide everything: start mid-action, never with a logo.
- Film vertical, natural light, real hands — polished ads underperform on organic.
- Reuse winning hooks with different footage rather than inventing new ones.

### Compression packing cubes

| Angle | Hook (first line on screen) |
|-------|------------------------------|
| Before/after | "My entire week fits in a carry-on and here's proof" |
| Challenge | "Packing 14 outfits into one cabin bag. Day 3 of trying" |
| POV story | "POV: you refuse to pay Jetstar $65 for a checked bag" |
| Satisfying | Slow zip compression shot, no talking, trending audio |

### Fascia recovery ring

| Angle | Hook |
|-------|------|
| Pain point | "If your calves feel like rocks after leg day, watch this" |
| Demo | Ring rolling down forearm/calf — visually satisfying, no words needed |
| Skeptic | "I thought this was a gimmick. My physio disagreed" |
| Routine | "3 minutes after every run. Non-negotiable" |

*Note: never make medical claims ("cures", "treats") — stick to "feels
amazing", "part of my recovery routine".*

### Pet grooming/bath brush

| Angle | Hook |
|-------|------|
| Reaction | Dog melting into the brush — pure reaction content, always works |
| Before/after | "The amount of fur this pulled off my 'non-shedding' dog" |
| Relatable | "POV: your dog hears the brush come out" |
| ASMR | Brushing sounds + purring cat, no voiceover |

## 3. Measuring the test (from Business Plan Phase 3)

Per product, after ~2 weeks of daily posting:
- **Kill it** if no video broke 5,000 views and profile clicks are near zero.
- **Keep going** if any video broke ~20k views or you got add-to-carts.
- **Winner signal**: sales from organic alone → this is the product that earns
  the first paid-ads budget.
